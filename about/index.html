My story

Why I built EcomKeeping the way I did

Why e-commerce, specifically

I shop online. I understand the buyer side of what my clients build. But what drew me to this work was the seller side. Amazon settlements, Shopify payouts, FBA fee structures, COGS for inventory businesses, and US sales tax nexus. I studied these as a complete and interconnected system.

I chose to specialize because I have no interest in competing against generalists on price. My focus is narrow on purpose. The narrower the focus, the deeper the expertise. A generalist bookkeeper adapting to e-commerce does it after learning everything else first. I learned e-commerce first and built the practice around it.

That difference matters. I do not need your help understanding what a settlement is. I do not need to look up how A2X works. I know your platform, your tools, and what goes wrong when they are not set up correctly.

What I want EcomKeeping to be

I am transparent about where I am. EcomKeeping is early-stage. I am building a practice, not running an established firm. What I bring is real certification, e-commerce-specific training, and a genuine commitment to doing this work properly.

The goal is not to scale to hundreds of clients. The goal is to serve 8 to 10 sellers so thoroughly that each one feels like the only client. Every client gets the full depth of expertise, full responsiveness, and full attention their business deserves.

I want you to see me as the accounting partner you trust completely with your finances. Not someone you have to double-check. Not someone who needs your help understanding your platform. Not someone you eventually outgrow. That is what EcomKeeping is built to be.

What I believe

The principles behind how I work

01

A2X automates data transfer. It does not replace judgment.

The software handles the mechanical work. Knowing whether a settlement posted correctly, whether COGS reflects a recent cost change, and whether the Balance Sheet makes sense still requires a trained specialist paying attention.

02

Your books should tell an accurate story every month, not just satisfy a CPA once a year.

If you are making pricing decisions based on a P&L where net deposits are recorded as revenue, you are running your business on fiction. Monthly bookkeeping exists to give you real numbers in real time.

03

Transparency builds more durable trust than inflated claims.

I do not fabricate testimonials or imply client history I do not have. My credentials are real. My training is real. My positioning is built on what I can actually deliver, not on what sounds most convincing.

04

Serving fewer clients well is worth more than serving many clients adequately.

A solo practice capped at 10 clients can give each one something a high-volume firm cannot: actual attention. When something needs your attention, you hear about it the same day.

05

Part of this job is education, not just execution.

A seller who understands their gross margin and what moves it makes better decisions than one who just receives a PDF. The Loom walkthroughs and plain-English summaries are part of the service, not extras.

06

Refer to professionals when the work is outside scope.

I am a bookkeeper, not a CPA, not a tax preparer, not a sales tax filing specialist. When your work needs those professionals, I say so clearly and help you get to the right person with clean books in hand.
